Section 1: Executive Summary

You have identified a critical distinction in data strategy: knowing where the data comes from (document sources) is far less useful for decision-making than knowing what the data functionally represents. Rather than slicing by document titles, the most effective way to map the Vision Memory is by Functional Strategic Domains. By categorizing the database's 1,455 entities based on the actual transformation initiatives and operational targets they represent, we reveal exactly where the Kingdom is focusing its execution capabilities.

Section 2: Slicing by Transformation Focus

Instead of looking at the 38 source documents, we can slice the repository's contents by analyzing the focus areas of the national initiatives it tracks. The database captures a massive scale of execution—specifically monitoring 1,502 total Vision Program Initiatives, of which 674 are completed and 596 are actively on track (Vision 2030 Annual Report 2025).

When we evaluate how these transformation data points are categorized internally (based on the DGA Meta Study 2024 tracking 490 foundational transformation initiatives), a clear functional slicing of the repository emerges:

  • Digital Platforms, Data & Core Systems (54.7%): The definitive majority of the strategic execution data is concentrated here. This includes targets for digital products, data management, IT governance, and core operational systems.
  • Strategy & Organization (16.7%): This slice contains the structural entities—organizational capabilities, governance frameworks, and strategic direction mandates.
  • Operational & Process Excellence (15.1%): Data points focused strictly on internal efficiencies, process automation, and operational health metrics.
  • Environmental, Social, and Governance - ESG (1.2%): The emerging baseline of sustainability targets and reporting metrics.
  • Other Specialized Domains (12.3%): The remaining distribution covering niche sectoral capabilities and localized targets.

Section 3: Strategic Value of this New Slicing

Visualizing the data through this functional lens completely changes how we use the Vision Memory. If our department is building a new capability, this slicing immediately tells us that the national agenda is overwhelmingly weighted toward Data Governance and Digital Platforms (over 54%). If we are proposing an operational capability, we can immediately drill into the 15.1% of the database dedicated to Process Excellence to find the exact national benchmarks we need to align with.

Section 4: Value Chain Dependencies

This functional distribution maps directly to how we execute projects. Our internal IT Systems and Process improvements (the "Build" phase of our strategy) directly inherit their targets from the Digital & Core Systems slice of the national data. By slicing the data functionally rather than by source document, we bridge the gap between abstract macroeconomic policies and tactical, deployable capabilities within our organization.
